# Manuscript submission guidance

Load this reference when selecting a reporting guideline, building a submission
package, or auditing venue-specific components.

## Determine the article type first

Requirements often differ within one journal. Confirm whether the manuscript is
an original research article, methods paper, data paper, brief report, review,
registered report, resource, software paper, or another named type.

Do not combine instructions from different article types.

## Build requirements from authoritative sources

For each consequential rule capture:

- exact requirement
- official URL
- access date
- article type
- whether it is mandatory or recommended
- current manuscript location
- action and status

Take screenshots or retain a local note when requirements are presented only
inside a submission system and the project needs an auditable record.

## Select reporting guidance

Use the study design and methods to identify applicable guidance. Possible
families include:

- observational studies
- randomised experiments
- systematic reviews and maps
- prediction models
- animal research
- qualitative research
- economic evaluation
- data and software papers
- discipline-specific extensions

Use an official registry or the guideline's own site where possible. A journal
checklist may supplement but does not replace the complete official guidance.

## Separate four kinds of compliance

| Type | Question |
|---|---|
| Scientific completeness | Is enough reported to understand and evaluate the work? |
| Reporting guideline | Are applicable checklist items reported and locatable? |
| Journal style | Does structure and presentation follow venue rules? |
| Technical package | Are every file, format, version, and declaration ready? |

Passing one does not imply passing the others.

## Common submission components

### Title page

Verify:

- exact manuscript title
- author names and preferred forms
- affiliations and mapping
- corresponding author
- present addresses when required
- word count, figure count, or table count when required
- running title
- acknowledgements or declarations if the venue places them here

### Declarations

Use authoritative project records for:

- ethics and permits
- consent where relevant
- funding and grant identifiers
- competing interests
- author contributions
- preregistration
- data, code, and materials access
- use of generative AI when disclosure is required

Never infer these from manuscript content.

### Supplements

Check that:

- each supplement is cited in the main text
- numbering is consistent
- legends and methods are sufficient
- supplementary results do not contradict the main manuscript
- files contain no stale drafts, comments, or identifying information
- data dictionaries or metadata accompany deposited data where needed

### Anonymisation

For double-blind review inspect:

- title page separation
- author names and affiliations
- acknowledgements
- self-identifying prose
- repository links
- ethics and location details that may identify the team
- file properties, comments, tracked changes, and embedded metadata

Follow the venue's exact policy. Do not remove scientifically necessary details
without a permitted anonymised substitute.

## Word-limit adaptation

Reduce in this order:

1. repetition across abstract, Results, and Discussion
2. background not needed for the gap
3. procedural history not needed for reproducibility
4. exact values already available in tables
5. secondary interpretation outside the central contribution
6. non-essential examples and qualifiers

Do not remove necessary methods, uncertainty, material limitations, eligibility
rules, or evidence boundaries merely to meet length. Escalate when the article
type cannot accommodate the work without scientific loss.

## Final consistency sweep

Cross-check exact strings and values across:

- title page and submission form
- abstract and main text
- Methods and reporting checklist
- Results, tables, figures, and supplements
- citations and reference list
- ethics, funding, contributions, and declarations
- availability statements and actual repository access
- filenames and in-text file references
